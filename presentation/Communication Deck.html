Pillar 01 · Active Listening

5 things great listeners do

🔇

Give full attention

Phone down, body turned toward the speaker.

🔁

Paraphrase back

"So what you're saying is…" to confirm.

❓

Ask, don't assume

Open questions beat filling in blanks.

⏸️

Let silence sit

A pause invites more than a quick reply.

👀

Read the body language

Tone & face often say more than words.

Try this today: in your next chat, paraphrase once before you reply.

Pillar 02 · Written & Digital

Write so they can't misread you

Lead with the ask

Put the point in the first line. Don't bury it.

Be specific

"By Friday 3pm," not "soon." Names, dates, numbers.

Mind the tone

No tone of voice online — read it out loud first.

Rule of thumb: if it would take more than 3 messages to sort out, just call or talk in person.

Pillar 03 · Feedback

A formula that takes the sting out

Click to build the S·B·I sentence, piece by piece.

S
Situation

"In yesterday's group meeting…"

When & where it happened

B
Behaviour

"…you took notes for everyone…"

What they actually did (facts, not labels)

I
Impact

"…which kept us all on the same page. Thank you!"

The effect it had on you / the team

CommunicationFeedback
Pillar 03 · Feedback

Receiving it without the armour

1 · Breathe. Your first reaction isn't your final one.
2 · Get curious. "Can you give me an example?"
3 · Say thanks. It took courage for them to tell you.
Feedback is information about the work, not a verdict on you.
